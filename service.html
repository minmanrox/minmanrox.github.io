Service

Research Lab

My work in the SeTH Lab at Texas A&M provides me the opportunity to provide to the academic community. As an undergraduate researcher, I work alongside graduate students in their efforts to advance hardware security. My role is to support their research products with working solutions and provide an extra set of hands on every project. I also help new students get up to speed with the tools they need and processes within the lab. The help that I provide is invaluable to the lab and the hardware security community as a whole.

Aggie Habitat

Aggie Habitat is the Texas A&M chapter of Habitat for Humanity. They provide many services for the community, including fundraising and construction. While I am not an official member of Aggie Habitat, I have participated in their events, and I have been greatly impressed by the service they provide and the impact they have on the community. I highly recommend them to anyone looking for service opportunities.

Eta Kappa Nu

I am the Corporate Chair for the Eta Kappa Nu (HKN) honor society at Texas A&M. HKN is a society for Electrical and Computer Engineering students that focuses on service, leadership, and scholarship. As Corporate Chair, I am responsible for organizing and leading the corporate events that HKN hosts. These events include company info sessions, networking events, and professional development workshops. I am also responsible for maintaining relationships with our corporate sponsors.
